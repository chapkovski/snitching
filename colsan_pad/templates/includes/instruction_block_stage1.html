Part 1:

  • Each period in Part 1 of the study consists of 4 stages.
  • In Stage 1 Each participant of type B receives in the beginning of each period an endowment of {{ Constants.principal_endowment }}.
  • Out of this amount Participant B sends to each of Participants A a fixed amount of {{ Constants.worker_wage }}.
  • {# todo: remove hardcoding #} In Stage 2 a Participant A chooses an effort level (any integer number between 0 and 7).
  • For each effort level chosen by participant A there is an associated cost of effort. The cost of effort can be found in the following table:
  • {% for i in Constants.cost_effort_table.keys %} {% endfor %} {% for i in Constants.cost_effort_table.values %} {% endfor %}
    Effort:{{ i }}
    Cost of effort:{{ i }}
  • The level of effort that you chooses is multiplied by {{ Constants.principal_coef }}, and added to a payoff of Participant B.

  • Participant A payoff in the end of each period is calculated as follows:

    Fixed amount received from Participant B - Cost of effort = Participant A payoff

  • Participant B payoff in the end of each round is calculated as follows::

    Initial Endowment - Sum of fixed amounts sent to Participants A + sum of efforts made by Participants A x {{ Constants.principal_coef }}

  • {# TODO: modify this example so its based on Constants not hardcoded#} For example if Participant A chooses a level of effort 5, that decreases their payoff by {{ Constants.cost_effort_table.5|c }} but increases the payoff of a Participant B by {{ 50|c }}.
  • Other Participants of type A face the similar choice. Their choice affects their own payoff and a payoff of a Participant B.
  • In Stage 3 each Participant A decides for each effort that other Participants A can make, whether they would like to inform Participant B about effort levels of these specific Participants.
  • In Stage 4 a Participant B observes a total sum of efforts made by all Participants A. One participant A is chosen randomly in each period and the decisions of this particular Participant A about informing a Participant B are implemented.
  • For example, if some Participant A chose an effort 5, and another randomly picked Participant A decides to inform Participant B about this, a Participant B will be informed about this effort level.
  • In stage 4 Participant B can send deduction tokens to any Participant A. Each deduction token sent by a Participant B reduces the payoff of the participant A by {{ Constants.pun_coef|c }}, but also decreases a payoff of a Participant B by {{ 1|c }}.
